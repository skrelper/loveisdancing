1. Love is dancing


Lyrics

Wrote it about the admiration I feel towards people who don't get anxiety about going to houseparties. My friend is a DJ and I find it magical how the crowd innately moves their bodies to the music. I had some of my friends help me record some stuff for a music video last October... but it didn't turn out how I wanted it to. And since then, I've come up with a better idea for it and a more concrete story... to an extent.

Was super into full-spectrum photography when ideating the video. If I could get my hands on a high quality full-spectrum video camera and some lenses then I would explore that more, but they're a little expensive and I don't know how often I'd use it.

Regardless, the certain visual aspects I want to explore mainly pertain to visuals I associate with this song. I have an idea to stick a headphone cord in my veins like a needle with some visual effects. Also, randomly thought of a large headphone stethoscope of a prop. Whether these come to fruition or not will be decided, would love to hear your thoughts on those!

It was ironic because I bought a brand new phone for better low-light capturing, but ended up liking a lot of the footage recorded on my old iPod a lot more. My favorite phrase in the song is "Love is music, dance is sex" per the shirt and jacket.

2. everything2me


Lyrics

This is the first song I made for the EP. It's a lot of fun in my mind and I picture the music video to be more eccentric and heavily leaning into surrealistic aspects rooted in the uprise of early 2000s technology. Not so much Frutiger Aero, but something like Juicy Drop Pop ads and just extreme color.

I remember being young and just being obsessed with Snapchat filters and recording my in-denial gay self acting fucking crazy. I look back and realize how much I eventually hated myself for acting like that, but now I fully embrace it and that's kind of what this song is about sonically. Lyrically, I guess it's more about my current worries.

I have big dreams for the music video to be recorded in an entirely white room where my current self (Love is dancing) stumbles upon my younger self (everything2me) and they start fighting. The fight takes place inside a Street Fighter-style video game.

The graphic below was the very first thing I made for this (without knowing). I love the subtlety of pictures I've taken peaking through and how you can tell the main subject is me, but there's just so much going on that I'm not entirely in the spotlight. As of now, I imagine the music videos to not have as much distortion or noise, but I do want to pull some inspiration from more abstract pieces of work.
